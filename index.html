Overview

Parfum Campaigns is an independent editorial archive focused on documenting fragrance campaign imagery across major houses, independent brands, and historical periods. The archive prioritizes visual structure, compositional intent, and campaign clarity over trend relevance or commercial performance.

Mission

The mission of Parfum Campaigns is to preserve and contextualize fragrance advertising as a distinct visual and cultural discipline, separate from retail, influencer media, and short-term promotional cycles.

Scope

Coverage includes print campaigns, billboards, film stills, outdoor advertising, and official brand imagery released in support of fragrance launches. Editorial commentary is limited to structural, visual, and historical context.

Site structure

The site is organized by brand, fragrance, year, and campaign type. Pages are designed for clarity, consistency, and long-term reference.

Image standards

All images use descriptive filenames, neutral presentation, and non-decorative alt text. Cropping and enhancement are limited to preservation and clarity.

SEO standards

SEO is treated as an archival function. Filenames, headings, and metadata are descriptive rather than promotional. No affiliate or tracking systems are used.

Style guide

Editorial tone is factual, restrained, and observational. The archive avoids hype language, ranking systems, and commercial calls to action.

Change log

Structural and editorial updates are logged when relevant to archival integrity.

References

Parfum Campaigns was initiated as an independent archival project by contributors involved in contemporary fragrance research and private fragrance development.

This site is maintained as a static editorial archive. All trademarks and campaign imagery remain the property of their respective owners.
