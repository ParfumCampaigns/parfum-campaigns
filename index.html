Overview

Parfum Campaigns is an independent editorial archive focused on documenting fragrance campaign imagery across major houses, independent brands, and historical periods. The archive prioritizes visual structure, compositional intent, and campaign clarity over trend relevance or commercial promotion.

Mission

The mission of Parfum Campaigns is to preserve and contextualize fragrance advertising as a visual and cultural record. The site does not sell products, publish reviews, or participate in affiliate marketing. Its sole function is documentation and reference.

Scope

The archive includes global luxury houses, designer brands, niche perfumers, and historically significant campaigns. Editorial selection is based on visual significance, campaign clarity, and cultural relevance.

Site structure

Parfum Campaigns operates as a static, single-page reference with anchored sections. Campaign entries are organized by brand and product, using consistent grids and standardized metadata.

Image standards

All images must use descriptive, SEO-friendly filenames and precise alt text. Decorative imagery is avoided; all images must serve a documentary or structural purpose.

SEO standards

SEO practices emphasize clarity, accuracy, and descriptive naming. No keyword stuffing, tracking scripts, or analytics are used. Indexing is passive and content-driven.

Style guide

Editorial tone is neutral, factual, and restrained. The archive avoids hype language, marketing copy, and subjective scoring.

Change log

Structural or editorial changes to the archive are logged here for transparency and historical reference.

References

References may include brand archives, campaign credits, photographer attribution, and historical advertising records.

Parfum Campaigns is an independent editorial project. No affiliation with brands listed.
